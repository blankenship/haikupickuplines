Your Mileage May Vary

Haiku Pickup Lines were for my girlfriend. Success! She is now my wife.

Haiku is a short form of poetry originally from Japan. The traditional haiku form consists of three lines in a 5/7/5 syllable pattern, including a seasonal reference, and a kireji or “cutting word” which gives structure to the poem, like closure at the end of the verse, or a pause in between thoughts in the middle of a verse.

While “haiku” is the widely-recognized name for these poems in the Western world, some purists insist using the 5/7/5 form to illustrate aspects of the human comedy and our idiosyncrasies is not true haiku, but senryū.

Pickup lines are a short form of poetry originally from lonely people. Traditional pickup lines are quick, ostensibly clever conversation openers intended to aid the speaker in engaging someone for conversation or romance.

I wrote the first Haiku Pickup Lines on a napkin at an airport bar, while watching a travel-weary businessman unsuccessfully flirt with a young flight attendant.
